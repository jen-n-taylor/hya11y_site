An Introduction to Web Accessibility

October 2016

Dave Newton

Who are you?

This is a seriously deep question that I’m not fully prepared to answer. But:

  • I’m Dave
  • I’ve been doing web dev for a long time
  • I’m old and cranky
  • I’m pessimistic about how things are but optimistic about how they can be

Where do you work?

Shopify! I am the front end development lead for sales channels, which are basically “places merchants can sell things”. E.g. Online Store, Point of Sale, Facebook, Pinterest, etc.

What do you do by day?

Because I’m a lead, a lot of my day-to-day is spent planning and organizing and helping set direction for my team. I also spend a bunch of time making sure they have what they need to do their best work, with as few frustrations as possible. Every once in a while I still get to write some code.

Why do you care about accessibility?

I don’t know… I’m colour blind, but I don’t think that has anything to do with it. Mostly I just care because to me it’s part of being good at my job — making an accessible website is just part of making a good website, and I want to make good websites.

Haris Mahmood

Who are you?

I’m Haris! I’m a fan of the internet, educating + education, kindness and Emma Watson.

Where do you work?

I work at Shopify as a front end developer, and at HackerYou as a lead instructor for the part-time intro and advanced web development courses.

What do you do by day?

I’m part of a small team at Shopify working on the Buy Button platform. I’m responsible for ensuring our front-end is performant, responsive, scalable, and accessible while maintaining the Shopify style guide.

Why do you care about accessibility?

As citizens of the world it can sometimes be difficult to bring about changes in government and policy. The web, however, is a world of its own, and a lot easier to change. We are its caretakers, rule makers, thought leaders and builders, and are all responsible for doing are part to make it an equally accessible platform for everyone.
