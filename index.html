HackerYou Web Accessibility Club

A meetup for junior developers working to make the web an inclusive space.

About the Club

The HackerYou Accessibility Club was founded by 2 students in HackerYou’s immersive bootcamp in the summer of 2016. Initially a conversational lunch gathering, it grew to more formally structured meetings after the founders graduated. The club aims to provide a safe learning environment for junior front-end developers to get a solid introduction to inclusive design and web accessibility practices.

We are proud to partner with the accessibility experts at a11yTO to achieve this goal.

Welcome to the World of WCAG 2.0!

When: Monday February 27

Where: Influitive, 111 Peter Street, 3rd Floor, Toronto, Ontario

Doors open at 6pm and we get started at 6:30pm.

You don’t need to be a WCAG wizard to write accessible code. You don’t even need to know that WCAG stands for Web Content Accessibility Guidelines. Just bring your laptop and join us for a code-along where we'll tackle accessibility "gotchas".

In this session you will learn:

  • How to use automated tools and manual tests to evaluate a site’s accessibility level
  • How to read an accessibility evaluation with reference to WCAG
  • The difference between WCAG levels A - AAA
  • How to find solutions to common accessibility issues through WCAG

How to prepare:

  • Bring your laptop, a powercord, and have your favorite text editor ready!
  • Make sure you have the Google Chrome browser downloaded.

Meet Your Screenreader!

When: April, 2017

Where: Location to be determined (check back soon!)

Doors open at 6pm and we get started at 6:30pm.

Testing your site for screen reader users can be difficult if you don't know how to use one. Bring your laptop (and your headphones) and we'll walk you through the ins and outs of using and testing sites with a screen reader.

In this session you will learn:

  • A hands-on session geared towards getting comfortable with a screenreader (VoiceOver for Mac userse or NVDA for Windows users)
  • How to navigate a website with a screen reading device
  • How to find and implement solutions to common issues for screen reader users

How to prepare:

  • Bring your laptop, a powercord, and have your favorite text editor ready!
  • Make sure you have the Google Chrome browser downloaded.
